Quick Answers to Common Questions

The stuff I get asked all the time. For more detailed answers, check out the full FAQ.

🤔 Should I pay off debt or save first?

Depends on your interest rates. If your debt is over 7%, prioritize that (except your 401k match—always get that free money). Under 7%? Do both. And please, build a $1,000 mini emergency fund first so you don't go deeper into debt when life happens.

Read More →

💳 How do I improve my credit score fast?

Pay down your credit cards to under 30% of your limit (under 10% is even better). That's the quickest win. Then set up autopay so you never miss a payment. I went from 540 to 720 in 18 months doing just these two things.

Read More →

📈 How much should I have in my emergency fund?

Start with $1,000 if you have debt. Once debt-free, aim for 3-6 months of essential expenses. If you have kids or are self-employed, lean toward 6 months. Single with a stable job? 3 months is probably fine.

Read More →

🎯 What's the best budgeting method?

The one you'll actually stick with. I started with 50/30/20 (simple), moved to zero-based (more control), and now do a hybrid. Try one for a month. If it feels like torture, try another. There's no gold star for suffering.

Read More →

🏦 Where should I keep my emergency fund?

High-yield savings account at an online bank. Currently earning 4-5% vs 0.01% at traditional banks. I use Marcus and Ally, but there are lots of good options. Keep it liquid but separate from your checking so you're not tempted.

Read More →

💰 When should I start investing?

Yesterday. But seriously—get any employer 401k match first (that's a 100% return), pay off high-interest debt, build a small emergency fund, then start investing. Even $50/month is better than waiting until you have "enough."
