About MoneyMind

A small personal finance site maintained by Jake Miller.

Why I write this site

I started MoneyMind after rebuilding my own day-to-day finances: tracking spending, paying down debt, creating a small emergency fund, and learning how to invest without pretending I understood every acronym on day one.

The point of the site is not to sound like a bank brochure. I write for readers who want plain examples, small next steps, and honest trade-offs when money is tight or confusing.

What MoneyMind covers

  • Budget routines that can survive busy weeks.
  • Debt payoff decisions, including when to slow down.
  • Emergency funds and irregular expenses.
  • Beginner investing concepts in plain English.
  • Money conversations with partners and families.

What this site does not do

MoneyMind does not provide personalized financial, tax, investment, or legal advice. Articles are educational and based on general experience. If a decision could affect taxes, retirement, legal obligations, or major assets, talk with a qualified professional who can review your situation.

Updates and corrections

I review older articles when a reader flags unclear wording, a broken link, a changed product detail, or an example that needs better context. Send corrections through the contact page and include the article URL plus the section that needs review.
