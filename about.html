Hi, I'm Jake

I messed up my finances so you don't have to. Here's my story of going from $40K in debt to financial freedom—and why I started this blog.

My Story (The Messy Truth)

Back in 2019, I was 28 years old and completely broke. Not "I can't afford avocado toast" broke—I mean "my car got repossessed" broke. I had $23,000 in credit card debt, $17,000 in student loans, and a credit score of 540. I was making $45,000 a year as a marketing coordinator, but somehow I was always behind on bills.

The breaking point? My girlfriend (now wife) wanted to talk about our future. She asked about my finances, and I realized I had no idea where my money was going. I was living in denial, paying minimums on credit cards while spending $200 a month on takeout and $150 on a gym membership I never used.

So I did something radical: I started tracking every single dollar. For three months, I wrote down everything I spent. It was embarrassing. I was spending $800 a month on restaurants and bars alone. But seeing the numbers in black and white changed everything.

I started this blog in 2021, two years into my debt-free journey, because I wanted to share what actually worked (and what didn't). No fluff, no judgment—just real talk about money from someone who's been there.

What You'll Find Here

This isn't your typical finance blog. You won't find me using words like "fiscal responsibility" or "portfolio diversification" without explaining what they actually mean. Instead, you'll get:

  • Real numbers from my actual budget (including the mistakes)
  • Step-by-step guides that don't assume you have a finance degree
  • Honest reviews of financial products I've actually used
  • Stories from readers who've paid off debt and built wealth
  • No shame, no judgment—just practical advice that works

My Promise to You

I'll never pretend to be perfect. I still make money mistakes. I still have months where I overspend. But I've learned how to recover quickly and stay on track. And I'll share every lesson with you.

If you're drowning in debt, living paycheck to paycheck, or just want to get better with money—you're in the right place. Take a deep breath. It's going to be okay. We've got this.

My Financial Journey: The Highlights

2019

Rock Bottom

$40,000 in debt. Credit score of 540. Living paycheck to paycheck. Car transmission died and I had $47 to my name. This was my wake-up call.

2020

The Turnaround Begins

Started tracking every dollar. Paid off first credit card. Built a $1,000 emergency fund. Credit score jumped to 620.

2021

Debt-Free & Blogging

Finally debt-free after 2.5 years. Started MoneyMind to share what I learned. Credit score hit 680. Built 3-month emergency fund.

2022

Building Wealth

Maxed out 401(k) for the first time. Started Roth IRA. Savings rate hit 40%. Got married (she said yes despite my money mess!).

2023-2024

Growing & Learning

Savings rate reached 50%. Investment portfolio crossed $50,000. Started freelance writing side hustle. Credit score: 720.

2025-2026

Still Going

Investment portfolio crossed $80,000. House hunting begins (exciting and terrifying). Started this site to document everything. Still learning something new about money every single day.

Questions People Ask Me

Are you a financial advisor?

Nope. I'm just a regular guy who messed up his finances and figured out how to fix them. I share what worked for me, but I'm not a professional. Always do your own research and consider talking to a professional for your specific situation.

How do you make money from this blog?

Through affiliate partnerships and display advertising. When you click certain links and make purchases, I may earn a commission at no extra cost to you. I also have display ads. But I never recommend something just because it pays—my integrity is worth more than any commission. Read my full disclosure.

What's your net worth?

As of January 2026, around $95,000 (including investments, emergency fund, and retirement accounts). Not impressive compared to some FIRE bloggers, but I started from negative $40,000. Progress is progress.

Do you ever splurge?

Absolutely. I just budget for it. My wife and I take one nice vacation a year, we eat out once a week, and I buy good coffee beans. The key isn't never spending—it's intentional spending.

What's your biggest money regret?

Not starting earlier. I wasted my 20s being terrible with money. If I'd started at 22 instead of 28, I'd be so much further ahead. But regretting the past doesn't help—only action does.

Can I ask you a personal question?

Sure. Email me. I read every message personally and try to respond to everyone. Can't promise I'll have all the answers, but I'm happy to help where I can.
